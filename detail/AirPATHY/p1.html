Project Description

In a visionary, speculative world-building film, Manila, the capital of the Philippines, is portrayed as undergoing a spectacle of green transformation. The narrative unfolds in a future where urban redevelopment integrates with nature, presenting a city revitalised by innovative and sustainable design strategies. Drawing inspiration from Bruce Sterling's "Shaping Things," the film envisions a convergence of technology and biology, introducing the concept of “Gizmology.” Through a series of gizmos, urban landscapes flourish with anti-fishbone forestation corridors and top-down typology.

Under the support of ECOIN, a global conglomerate dedicated to transforming cities into more "breathable" spaces, the political intervention has fostered a new ideology among citizens: Economics = Ecology. This paradigm shift aims to tackle deteriorating living conditions exacerbated by overpopulation. Building upon the foundations laid by the Migrant Workers and Overseas Filipinos (OFW) Act of 1995, the newly proposed E-OFW Act of 2025 strives to alleviate urban density and enhance living standards.

However, the ambitious idea comes with a high cost. As a reaction to climate change conditions in the region, the film narrates how several urban and architectural interventions could alter the lives, memories and hopes of different citizens. From the personal scale to the broader urban landscape, the city becomes an intelligent, highly complex and multi-layered place.
